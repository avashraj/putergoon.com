I’ve been paying for Shonen Jump so I read a bunch of manga recently. This is gonna be some quick glazes on my two favorites that are currently monthly releases. **Spoilers for Phantom Busters and Bug Ego ahead.**

### Phantom Busters

So basically, spirits exist and the evil ones need to be exorcised (via the main character’s digestive system). Phantom Busters portrays four high school boys who exorcise ghosts in their titular club, Phantom Busters. At the same time, Mogari (the main main character) seeks to have as much fun as possible in high school. Mogari comes from the most goated clan or whatever and his powers gives him the ability to eat ghosts to exorcise them. Consuming ghosts has been compared to “eating people” and I think routinely having the protagonist seemingly cannibalize is a funny trope.

Phantom Busters has a strong main cast. Each of the four main characters are unique (smart guy Eugene, edgy guy Kanzaki, shy guy Tamon, stupid guy Mogari) with specific roles within their attempted exorcisms and their social circle. Their individuality compliments each other well and actually appears like a real friend group which many shounen can't portray well. Reading it actually feels like you’re watching a group of friends just mess around, which improves upon the slice of life elements of the story.

Speaking of the genre, the combination of shounen and slice of life brings great variety to the subplots of each chapter. I really enjoy the stupidity of their antics in the school life chapters as well as the potential drama it brings. For example, Kanzaki struggling with playing the guitar was a great arc not only for his character, but the group dynamic as well. Combining a scenario like a culture festival with Mogari running away from his goated clan made a fun read with an interesting dichotomy of conflicts.

Phantom Buster’s casual story, its refusal to take itself too seriously, and its great cast make for an enjoyable read. Its only 31 chapters too!

**8/10!**

### Bug Ego

In Bug Ego’s world, people can perform hacks. A hack is a series of intricate actions that causes a specific supernatural event to occur. I put off reading this for a while because I thought the title and cover art looked dumb and the plot itself sounds dull but I swear it's really worth it to read. Both the storyteller and the artist’s creativity seem to be on meth and are on full display in every chapter in every moment. A good example is the art in the third chapter with a clone of Hitsujiya conscience riding on a turd to reach his body after his original self pretends to be a vaccum cleaner to free himself. Read that last sentence again. What the hell am I even talking about. How does ONE come up with material like this?? Not only is the concept absurd, the art for this situation is some of the most beautiful drawings I’ve seen in manga especially for such a ludicrous situation. I have not read such an imaginative manga in a long time.

In addition to the creativity, I think Bug Ego’s second strongest asset are the main three characters. Kokudo is the boy who first discovered the hacks and showed them to Hitsujiya, a classmate of his. While reserved at extensively using the hacks at first, Kokudo realizes that he must use and research the “hacks” to actually understand them and keep himself and his friend safe (with this being realistic cope to keep having supernatural fun). I thought it was really funny (he’s like 4 feet tall btw) how he used a hack to try to make himself taller despite his early apprehensions at using the hacks. I think this was incredibly realistic (I'm 5'7 btw) and I don’t think it's a concept that is shown much in shounen where a main character actually uses a “get 6 pack in 10 minutes” method. I think Kokudo also had the first ethical, non-aura-farming aura moment in recent shounen when he saved Hitsujiya’s mind from floating into space.

Hitsujiya is the secondary protagonist. He's a bit dumb and tries to always act "cool". I think he’s the weakest out of the three characters, but he does have his highlights. Some of his defining moments were when he threw Kokudo into the dream escape hole to fight the devil on his own or when he entered the alternate dimension to get Canner back. He can’t help himself but help people in a genuine fashion and his realistic idealism contrast well with the other characters' pragmatism.

The last character in the main trio is Yuki. She's an intelligent girl who lost her curiosity of life due to (her parents) and the monotony of modern living. She hates everyone and constantly imagines everyone around her exploding until she activates a hack unintentionally with the other two boys. From there she proactively investigates the hacks and makes more progress than the other boys ever do. I think seeing the immediate growth in someone’s character by having the impossible revealed to them is really cool and she is probably my favorite character in the series due to her competency in comparison to the others.

Bug Ego only has 20 chapters right now so you should totally read it now and be super early and put everyone on!
I'm telling you I've never read a manga like this before.

**9/10!**
